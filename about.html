About Hilary

A long becoming —
not a single moment.

I am an emerging psychotherapist and writer based in Vancouver, British Columbia, completing my Master of Arts in Counselling Psychology at Yorkville University.

My desire to become a psychotherapist began in my early teens. Growing up as an only child to a mother navigating her own mental health journey, I found myself moving early into a role of emotional attunement — learning to read what was unspoken, to sit with difficulty, and to stay present when things were hard. What could have felt like a burden became, over time, a calling.

In my early twenties I entered therapy, and I have continued ever since. That work has been genuinely life-changing. The depth of self-understanding, the healing of old patterns, and the capacity for growth that good therapeutic work makes possible has shaped everything about how I approach this field. I do not come to counselling from the outside looking in. I know what it feels like to finally say the thing that has never been said — and to find that the person across from you does not flinch.

My professional path deepened that foundation. As COO of The Grief and Trauma Healing Centre and Co-Founder and CEO of P.A.T.H. Therapy, I worked alongside clinicians in intake, case consultation, and client-facing support across two distinct counselling environments in Vancouver and across BC. I observed firsthand how the quality of early therapeutic contact shapes everything that follows. I have intentionally stepped back from executive leadership to focus fully on my clinical training.

I am a traveler, a lover of the natural world, and someone who believes deeply that humans are love at their core. I write about the areas of therapy I find most compelling — EMDR, somatic and body-based approaches, Internal Family Systems and parts work, psychedelic-assisted therapy, couples counselling, and the intersection of self-compassion and identity. My practice opens in Vancouver in January 2027, offering in-person sessions and virtual counselling across British Columbia and Canada.

A belief

Humans are love at their core. Everything else is a layer.

This is not a soft idea. It is a clinical conviction — one that shapes how I sit with people, how I understand symptoms and defences, and how I hold hope for change even when it feels impossible.
